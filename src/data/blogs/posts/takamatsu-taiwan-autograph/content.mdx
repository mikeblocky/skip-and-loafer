# [TiCA25] Skip and Loafer creator Misaki Takamatsu’s autograph session: Portraying youth and breaking stereotypes of "cuteness"


---

Sharp Point Press invited Misaki Takamatsu, the creator of the manga Skip and Loafer, to Taiwan for an autograph session at the Taipei International Comics and Animation Festival (TiCA) on February 9. In addition to the festival, she held two more signing events on February 10 at the Animate Taipei store and Eslite Ximen store. This gave even more fans the opportunity to interact with the author.

![Autograph session event photo](/blog/takamatsu-interview-taiwan-tour/1.jpg)

During the TiCA event, Takamatsu first thanked the fans in attendance. She expressed that while their time together was short, she was very happy to have the chance to chat with everyone. Having arrived in Taipei the day before the event, she visited the National Palace Museum for the first time and loved the experience. "Unfortunately, the Jadeite Cabbage wasn't on display this time," she shared, "but I did get to see the famous Meat-Shaped Stone. If I have the chance, I will definitely visit Taiwan again!" When asked which of her characters' high school lives she would choose to experience, she picked Mukai. She explained that he is fairly cool, masculine, and has a very balanced personality, adding that living out that kind of youth sounds pretty nice.

![Misaki Takamatsu drawing for Taiwanese readers](/blog/takamatsu-interview-taiwan-tour/2.jpg)

Before the event began, Takamatsu sat down for a media interview to share more behind-the-scenes details about her creative process. Skip and Loafer heavily focuses on interpersonal relationships, including friendship, romance, and family dynamics. When asked how she structures the plot and balances these character interactions, Takamatsu explained that while it is technically a shoujo manga, her own student memories were not solely about romance. School life also involved club activities, school festivals, and pouring her heart into the things she loved. She also gathered input from various people, realizing that everyone has completely different ways of thinking and approaching life. As a result, she does her best to strike a natural balance between all these different elements.

![Back view of Takamatsu](/blog/takamatsu-interview-taiwan-tour/3.jpg)

Regarding the manga's Japanese title, Skip and Loafer, she explained that including "Loafer", a shoe commonly worn by students, was meant to evoke a high school atmosphere. Meanwhile, "Skip" represents a lighthearted, bounding spirit that refuses to be bogged down by minor things. She noted that while the two words might seem disconnected at first glance, she felt it would be wonderful if the combination successfully captured the feeling of youth.

![Title and youth theme image](/blog/takamatsu-interview-taiwan-tour/4.jpg)

When asked how she views the characters she created, she stated that rather than seeing them as her own children, she considers them independent individuals. Because there is a significant age gap between her and the characters, she looks at them as being half child and half adult. She mentioned that she does not have any specific messages she wants to tell her characters directly. However, if she had to say something, it would probably be along the lines of "It is okay even if you fail, because that is part of growing up." This is also a core message she hopes to convey to her readers through the manga.

![Character philosophy image](/blog/takamatsu-interview-taiwan-tour/5.jpg)

Regarding character development, Takamatsu mentioned that she generally maps out the emotional trajectories of the cast right from the beginning. For example, although characters like Shima and Kazakami are very attractive and cool, she intentionally gave them realistic human flaws. She wanted to show that they are not flawless beings and can actually be disappointing at times. Through these subtle details, she hopes readers can see how these seemingly perfect characters react and grow when things do not go exactly as planned.

The manga often touches upon the nostalgic flavors of a character's hometown. Takamatsu shared that she tried soup dumplings (xiaolongbao) during her trip to Taiwan, and it made her think about how everyone has certain delicious foods tied to their memories. While cities offer plenty of trendy, high-end dining options, she believes people will always have moments where they crave simple, comforting food. She also shared a touching story about a dumpling shop she had once featured in her work. Even though the shop has since closed, she received a letter from the owner's son mentioning that he had read her manga, along with a photo of the shop's dumplings. She was deeply moved by the gesture.

![Food and nostalgia image](/blog/takamatsu-interview-taiwan-tour/6.jpg)

Unlike typical shoujo manga heroines, the protagonist of Skip and Loafer, Mitsumi, does not have a conventionally cute appearance. However, her actions and expressions radiate a genuine, pure charm. Takamatsu said that if readers feel that way about Mitsumi, it is the greatest compliment. She never once considered Mitsumi to be anything less than adorable, emphasizing that cuteness can be expressed in countless ways. Through this character, she hopes to break the general public's stereotypes of what it means to be "cute." When it comes to designing the characters' outfits and hairstyles, she envisions the types of clothes or styles they would personally like. She even does research to determine the most fitting looks for each of them.

![Mitsumi drawing at the end of signing session](/blog/takamatsu-interview-taiwan-tour/7.jpg)

When discussing her storyboarding and emotional delivery, Takamatsu explained that she allocates panel space based on the emotional peaks and valleys of the narrative. Scenes that require readers to feel a deep emotional impact are drawn meticulously, while other moments are intentionally kept brief. When asked which part of the story has left the deepest impression on her so far, Takamatsu pointed to the brief separation between Mitsumi and Shima, a scenario she had wanted to draw for a long time. As she mentioned earlier, she wanted these seemingly flawless individuals to experience a sense of loss, which adds authenticity to the characters. At the time, she was actually worried that the audience might start to dislike Shima, so she was quite surprised to see that readers ended up loving his character even more.

![Storyboarding and emotional peaks image](/blog/takamatsu-interview-taiwan-tour/8.jpg)

Speaking about the anime adaptation, she felt that the animated version perfectly captured the emotions she wanted to convey in the manga, which gave her a great sense of relief. However, when asked if she could perform the dance from the anime's opening theme, she laughed, crossed her arms to form an "X," and confessed that she cannot dance. She specifically highlighted the scene in the very first episode where Mitsumi is running. While it only took up a single page in the manga, the anime depicted the character's desperate effort in such meticulous detail that it deeply moved her.

Finally, she expressed her immense joy regarding the manga's international publication and how thrilled she was to interact with everyone. She concluded by once again thanking all the readers for their continuous support of Skip and Loafer.
